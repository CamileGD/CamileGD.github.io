Introduction to me

My name is Camile Cazot, I'm a passionate indie game developer, but most importantly, I'm all about video game art; not only the graphic aspect of it but also the video game as an art itself.

After finishing my 2 year Video Game Development studies in A+, I struggled to find what exactly I wanted to do. I love game design and the art in videogames, but what is the art in video games? Is it the sprites? Is it the code? Is it the music? The possibility of choice? The story telling? Of course that the answer varies on each personm, in my case, I found the beauty of video games in smooth movement, interesting visuals and a harmonic combination of both. Once I realized what I wanted to do, I decided to become a Game Designer, but I also want to be a Technical Artist, so I'm going to be both. And this portfolio reflects the things I'm doing to reach those goals.

Work

Lights Out

Lights out was a game created as a 4-month final project as part of a course during my studies in Video Game Development. We were a team composed of three: an artist, a programmer and me.

What was my role in this project? I carried out the art and level design, the technical art aspect and the majority of the code. The most interesting aspect of this project was the challenge of making a 2D pixel game in a 3D environment with dynamic lights.

Domingos Kombat

This game was a commission done as a side project along with the co-creators of Lights Out.

My role on this project mainly consisted in programming the combat and players. As we didn't have a say in the artistic design area, we only worked on the technical aspect.

Avril Labanshee

Avril Labanshee was created in the frame of "2021 Global Game Jam".

I worked as a designer and programmer, but I also helped with the game's art.
